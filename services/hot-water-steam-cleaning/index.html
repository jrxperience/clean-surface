Common questions

Steam cleaning FAQs.

When is steam cleaning better than regular pressure washing?

Steam cleaning is the better choice when grease, gum, oil stains, or embedded grime are involved. The heat breaks down the molecular bond between the contaminant and the surface in a way that cold water cannot replicate, even at very high pressure.

What kinds of jobs benefit most from hot-water cleaning?

Greasy driveways, restaurant and food-service entries, dumpster pads, gum-covered walkways, industrial concrete, and any surface with heavy oil buildup. It is also commonly used as a pretreatment step before applying concrete sealers.

Can steam cleaning be combined with other services?

Yes, and it often works best that way. For a job that includes a driveway, patio, and house exterior, the crew can apply steam to the concrete and oil-heavy areas while using soft washing on the delicate surfaces — all in the same visit.
