What is the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing uses high water pressure and is best suited for harder surfaces like concrete driveways, sidewalks, and commercial flatwork. Soft washing uses lower pressure combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions, making it the safer method for stucco, painted exteriors, trim, and other delicate surfaces that high pressure could damage.

When do you use hot-water steam cleaning?

Hot-water steam cleaning at 240°F is the stronger option for grease, gum, oil stains, and heavier buildup that cold water struggles to break down. It is commonly used on commercial concrete, dumpster pads, heavily stained driveways, and any surface where standard pressure washing falls short.

Can you remove oil stains, rust marks, and hard-water buildup?

Often significantly, yes. Oil and grease stains respond well to pretreatment followed by hot-water steam cleaning. Rust and hard-water marks can often be improved with the right treatment, though very old or deeply set stains may lighten rather than disappear completely. Will pressure washing damage my stucco or painted surfaces?

Not when done correctly. Stucco, painted walls, and trim are cleaned with soft-wash techniques that use lower pressure and appropriate cleaning agents. High pressure on those surfaces can damage finish, dislodge caulk, and force water into gaps — which is why Clean Surface does not apply standard pressure-washing settings to delicate finishes.

How often should a home be professionally cleaned in Arizona?

Most Phoenix-area homes benefit from annual exterior washing. Driveways, patios, pool decks, and commercial surfaces often need service more frequently — every six months in some cases — because of foot traffic, vehicle use, and how quickly dust and staining build up in the desert climate.

How long does a typical job take?

A standard house washing takes about 2–3 hours. A full exterior package including the driveway, patio, and windows can take 3–5 hours. Commercial jobs vary depending on square footage and surface complexity. Steve can give you a more specific estimate once he knows what surfaces are involved.

Do I need to be home for exterior cleaning?

Not always. Exterior-only work can usually be handled without you present as long as access, water connection, and any gate or entry requirements are confirmed in advance. Steve recommends being available for the initial walkthrough and the final inspection when possible.

Are the cleaning agents safe for plants and pets?

Yes. Clean Surface uses biodegradable, phosphate-free detergents that meet Arizona Department of Environmental Quality guidelines. Plants near the work area are pre-wetted or covered when needed, and surfaces are safe for pets and children once dry.

Do you clean windows too?

Yes. Clean Surface provides residential and commercial window cleaning that can include interior and exterior glass, frames, tracks, sills, and screens. Window cleaning is a natural add-on to house washing or commercial exterior service.

Can multiple services be bundled into one visit?

Yes. It is common to bundle driveway cleaning, house washing, patio or pool deck cleaning, and window cleaning into one job so the full property gets addressed at the same time. Bundling also tends to work better logistically since the crew and equipment are already on site.
